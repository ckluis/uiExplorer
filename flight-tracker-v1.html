Flight Tracker

v1 Opus 4.6 Fully Autonomous

Purpose

A FlightRadar24-meets-NASA Mission Control dark-mode flight tracking dashboard with a CSS-only world map built from clip-path continent outlines, animated flight paths, split-flap departure board typography, and detailed flight info panels with conic-gradient instrument gauges. Fills a gap — zero transportation, travel, or spatial visualization experiments existed in the portfolio. Demonstrates that complex spatial/geographic interfaces can be achieved with pure CSS.

Prompt

This version was generated autonomously. The prompt shown is a summary of the agent's intent, not a verbatim human prompt.

The originating user prompt was:

"Evaluate the project and recommend the next major app to add. It should be as substantial as the CRM. We want to build out a team of agents for this approach. Use an world class product manager to do the research and synthesis and the potential app options. Make a decision and then orchestrate the work and manage the work with a team of experts. In this case, we do not need an architect because this is purely html/css, but we do need the worlds best designer AND a UX fanatatic. Use this team to build at least 3 separate new apps and orchestrate their own sub-agents as necessary."

A PM research agent analyzed all 19 existing experiments and identified transportation/spatial visualization as a completely unrepresented domain. The Flight Tracker was selected as one of three new experiments, pushing CSS-only design into cartographic territory with clip-path continent maps and offset-path flight animations.
